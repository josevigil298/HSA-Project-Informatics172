Are You Graduating Soon? Worried About Health Finances?

About Us:

We are a team of Health Informatics students dedicated to improving access to health and financial planning tools for young adults, helping them understand and prepare for Health Savings Account (HSA) eligibility. We recognize that the transition from college to the workforce can be overwhelming, especially when it comes to understanding health insurance and financial responsibilities. Most existing financial tools are designed for established professionals, leaving students with little guidance as they prepare for independence. We created this platform to provide personalized guidance that helps students determine their HSA eligibility and take the steps needed to get started.

Purpose:

Many students are unsure of when they can open a Health Savings Account (HSA) or how to prepare for one. Our platform simplifies this process with personalized pathways based on tax and insurance status, guiding users through educational modules and an eligibility questionnaire. We aim to make the HSA process clear, actionable, and confidence-building.

HEALTH SAVINGS ACCOUNT

Why Should Post-Grads Prepare
for an HSA?

Combatting Financial Anxiety

Nearly 60% of Gen Z and Millennials report anxiety over money.

Practice primary prevention against anxiety by securing a dedicated, tax-free fund for medical costs through an HSA, so you’re prepared before a bill ever arrives.

The Triple-Zero Tax Advantage

Most savings accounts are taxed for every deposit, growth, or care.

An HSA is the only account where the IRS gets nothing:

  • $0 Tax on Deposits: Every dollar you put in is not taxed.
  • $0 Tax on Growth: Any interest you earn stays in your account.
  • $0 Tax on Care: Qualified medical spending stays tax-free.

Future-Proofing Your Health

Post-grad life is full of job changes — and most health coverage doesn’t follow you when you leave.

Unlike employer-tied insurance, an HSA is portable. It stays with you, ensuring that no matter where your career takes you, your ability to afford care remains constant.
