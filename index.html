Are You Graduating Soon? Want To Save Money?

The transition from college to the real world is one of the most stressful life events a young adult can face, especially regarding health and finances. Most existing financial tools are designed for established professionals, leaving students in a 'pre-eligibility' limbo where they lack the guidance to start early. While the focus of healthcare is often on treating illness, we believe that financial wellness is preventative care. We’ve seen how the complexity of postgraduate life can lead to "administrative burden" and health-avoidance behaviors, so we built this platform to bridge the gap between graduation and long-term health stability.

We aim to equip the next generation of professionals with the tools and health-literacy required to navigate High-Deductible Health Plans (HDHPs) and Health Savings Accounts (HSAs). By introducing HSAs early, we aim to transform financial literacy into a proactive health strategy.

Why Should Post-Grads Prepare
for an HSA?

Combatting Financial Anxiety

Nearly 60% of Gen Z and Millennials report anxiety over money.

Practice primary prevention against anxiety by securing a dedicated fund for medical costs

The Triple-Zero Tax Advantage

An HSA is the only account where the IRS gets nothing.

  • $0 Tax on Deposits: Every dollar you put in is not taxed, meaning you keep more of your paycheck.
  • $0 Tax on Growth: Any interest you earn stays in your pocket.
  • $0 Tax on Care: When you use it for doctor's visits or prescriptions, you don’t pay a cent in taxes

Future-Proofing Your Health

Post-grad life is full of job changes.

Unlike employer-tied insurance, an HSA is portable. It stays with you, ensuring that no matter where your career takes you, your ability to afford care remains constant
