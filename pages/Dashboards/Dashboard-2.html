How To Prepare For An HSA!

PHASE 1: TAX INDEPENDENCE TEST

1
Identify your Independent Tax Year: When will you be able to provide more than 50% of your own financial support (rent, food, insurance, etc.)?
2
Ensure your guardian does not claim you as a dependent on their taxes.

PHASE 2: INSURANCE COMPATIBILITY

Check whether your plan is an HDHP.

If you do NOT have an HDHP:
3
Leave your current plan or wait for it to end.
4
Enroll in an HDHP through an employer or the health insurance marketplace.
5
Once enrolled, open an HSA through your bank or HSA Provider (e.g., Fidelity or Health Equity).
If you DO have an HDHP:
6
You may keep this plan, but you must first become tax-independent to open an HSA.

REMINDER: You must be both tax independent and enrolled in an HDHP to open an HSA

PHASE 3: THE PREP WORK

7
Set aside at least $1750 ($1700/individual in 2026) before switching health plans ⓘ
8
You may start setting assigned a small monthly amount to build your deductible buffer.
9
Keep a medical paper trail to track healthcare spending/needs.
