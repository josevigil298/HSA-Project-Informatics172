You’re almost there,
one step to unlock your HSA!

You have your own HDHP plan, which is great. The only thing standing between you and your HSA is your tax filing status. Once you file independently, you’ll be fully eligible to contribute.

YOUR ACTION PLAN
✓

Confirm you have your own HDHP Coverage

Your health insurance plan is classified as a High-Deductible Health Plan and you are the primary policyholder — not covered under a parent’s plan

✓

Verify you pay more than 50% of your own living expenses

Rent, food, transportation, and insurance all count. If you cover the majority of your own costs, you likely qualify to file independently

3

Identify your first Independent Tax Year

This is the first year in which neither you nor your guardian claims dependent status for you. Once you’ve filed independently for that year, your HSA eligibility begins — you can open and contribute to your account immediately.

Tax Year Calculator →
4

File your taxes independently

You can file for free through IRS Free File if your income is under $79,000. Once your independent return is submitted, you’re officially HSA-eligible for that tax year.
