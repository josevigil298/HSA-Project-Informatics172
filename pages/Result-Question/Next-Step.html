YOUR PATHWAY

You’re almost there,
one step to unlock your HSA!

You file taxes independently and are insured, which is great. The only thing standing between you and your HSA is being enrolled in an HDHP. Once you change your plan, you’ll be fully eligible to contribute.

PHASE 1: TAX INDEPENDENCE TEST

Your tax filing status must be independent.

PHASE 2: INSURANCE COMPATIBILITY

Leave your current plan or wait for it to end.

Your current plan is not an HDHP.

Enroll in an HDHP through an employer or the health insurance marketplace.

Once enrolled, you can open your HSA.

Reminder: You must be both tax independent and enrolled in an HDHP to open an HSA.

PHASE 3: THE PREP WORK

Build a deductible buffer: Open a High-Yield Savings Account and save at least $1750 ($1700/individual in 2026).

The IRS minimum deductible for HSA-eligible plans updates annually.

You may start setting a small monthly amount to build your deductible buffer.

Keep a medical paper trail to track healthcare spending/needs.
